# Content Strategy: Assurance-Based Repositioning

**Document Purpose**: Strategic guidance for repositioning Speeki's interactive checklists from a consulting/advisory tone to an assurance-based professional services positioning.

**Key Constraint**: As an auditing and assurance company, Speeki cannot provide advice or consulting recommendations. All content must be repositioned to reflect an assurance provider's perspective.

---

## Executive Summary

The current checklist pages contain language that suggests consulting services ("help you build", "implement for you", "need help with"). This language must be replaced with assurance-focused terminology that:
- Emphasises verification, assessment, and independent evaluation
- Positions users as doing the work themselves (with Speeki providing assurance)
- Highlights what auditors look for (red flags, evidence requirements, documentation standards)
- Educates without advising

---

## Part 1: Language Repositioning Guide

### Current Problems Identified

| Current Language | Problem | Assurance-Based Alternative |
|------------------|---------|----------------------------|
| "Need Help Selecting ESG Software?" | Suggests advisory service | "Preparing for ESG Software Assurance?" |
| "The Speeki Platform helps organisations build..." | Implies consulting | "The Speeki Platform enables independent verification of..." |
| "Streamline your compliance processes" | Advisory/consulting tone | "Meet documentation requirements for external assurance" |
| "build, manage and maintain their systems" | Hands-on advisory | "Support documentation and evidence requirements for assurance" |
| "Need Support with ISO 37001 Certification?" | Suggests consulting help | "Ready for ISO 37001 Certification Assurance?" |
| "prepare confidently for certification" | Coaching/advisory | "Meet the evidence standards auditors require" |

### Recommended Reframing Approach

**FROM (Consulting)**: "We help you implement controls"
**TO (Assurance)**: "We verify controls are operating effectively"

**FROM (Consulting)**: "Need help building your programme?"
**TO (Assurance)**: "Ready for independent assurance of your programme?"

**FROM (Consulting)**: "Let us guide your compliance journey"
**TO (Assurance)**: "Ensure your programme meets third-party verification standards"

## Part 3: Industry Red Flags - Auditor's Perspective

Adding "red flags" helps users understand how auditors and assurance providers evaluate these areas. This educational approach is appropriate for assurance firms.

### Recommended Red Flag Categories

For each checklist domain, add a section highlighting what auditors commonly flag as concerns:

---

### ISO 37001 Anti-Bribery Red Flags

**Documentation Red Flags**:
- No evidence of board-level oversight or approval of the anti-bribery policy
- Risk assessments that lack quantitative analysis or jurisdictional considerations
- Training records without evidence of completion or comprehension testing
- Third-party due diligence files without documented decision rationale

**Control Design Red Flags**:
- Gift/entertainment thresholds set without reference to local law or industry norms
- No segregation between those approving payments and those recording them
- Whistleblowing channels that report to operational management, not compliance
- No evidence of periodic control testing or internal audit coverage

**Operational Red Flags**:
- Cash payments in high-risk jurisdictions without enhanced approval
- Unusual commission structures (success fees over 15%, payments to third countries)
- Agent appointments without documented business rationale
- Hospitality spend patterns that spike before major contract decisions

---

### ISO 37002 Whistleblowing Red Flags

**System Design Red Flags**:
- Reporting channels not accessible anonymously
- Investigation function reports to management being investigated
- No defined escalation matrix for sensitive reports
- Case management system without access controls or audit trail

**Operational Red Flags**:
- Reports closed without documented investigation rationale
- Average investigation time exceeding 90 days without explanation
- Lack of reporter feedback loop (even if anonymous)
- No evidence of root cause analysis for substantiated cases

**Governance Red Flags**:
- Board receives only summary statistics, not case themes
- No review of cases involving senior management by independent parties
- Retaliation allegations not tracked or reported separately
- No periodic testing of channel effectiveness (e.g., dummy reports)

---

### GHG Reporting Red Flags

**Data Quality Red Flags**:
- Scope 3 calculations using industry averages for >80% of emissions
- No documentation of emission factor sources or vintage
- Activity data from self-reported supplier questionnaires without verification
- Year-on-year intensity improvements exceeding 15% without documented initiatives

**Methodology Red Flags**:
- Inconsistent organisational boundaries between years
- Market-based scope 2 claims without valid contractual instruments
- Carbon offset retirements claimed before project verification
- Biogenic emissions not separately disclosed

**Process Red Flags**:
- No formal review or sign-off process for emissions data
- Calculation spreadsheets without version control or change logs
- Lack of reconciliation between financial and operational control boundaries

---

### ESG Reporting Assurance Red Flags

**Data Management Red Flags**:
- Manual data aggregation from multiple disconnected sources
- No defined data ownership or accountability structure
- KPIs calculated differently across business units
- Historical data restated without documented rationale

**Governance Red Flags**:
- ESG metrics not subject to same controls as financial reporting
- Materiality assessment conducted without stakeholder input documentation
- Targets set without documented baseline methodology
- No internal audit or second-line review of ESG disclosures

**Disclosure Red Flags**:
- Forward-looking statements without stated assumptions
- Peer comparisons using non-standardised metrics
- Environmental claims not tied to verified programmes
- Social metrics lacking demographic or geographic disaggregation

---

### ESG Software Evaluation Red Flags

**When assessing software for assurance-readiness, auditors flag**:

**Audit Trail Red Flags**:
- No user-level access logs
- Data can be modified without change history
- Calculations not transparent or auditable
- No ability to "freeze" reporting periods

**Data Integrity Red Flags**:
- Manual overrides without approval workflow
- No validation rules on data input
- API integrations without error logging
- No reconciliation features between source and aggregated data

**Reporting Red Flags**:
- Cannot regenerate historical reports exactly as published
- No version control on report templates or methodologies
- Methodology documentation stored outside the system
- Limited/no GRI, SASB, or framework-specific tagging

---

### ISO 26000 Social Responsibility Red Flags

**Governance Red Flags**:
- Board composition does not include ESG expertise
- Stakeholder engagement conducted ad hoc, not systematically
- No documented due diligence process for social impacts

**Human Rights Red Flags**:
- Supply chain due diligence limited to tier 1 suppliers
- Grievance mechanisms not accessible to affected communities
- No evidence of remedy where impacts identified

**Labour Practice Red Flags**:
- Contractor workforce excluded from health and safety metrics
- No gender pay gap analysis or disclosure
- Training records incomplete for safety-critical roles

---

### ISO 37000 Governance Red Flags

**Board Effectiveness Red Flags**:
- No documented skills matrix or gap analysis
- Board evaluation not conducted independently
- Tenure of directors exceeds 9 years without documented justification

**Oversight Red Flags**:
- Risk committee meeting frequency less than quarterly
- No evidence of board challenge to management proposals
- Delegation of authority not formally documented

---

### ISO 37008 Investigations Red Flags

**Process Red Flags**:
- Investigation scope not documented at commencement
- No protocols for evidence preservation and chain of custody
- Investigator reports to subject of investigation

**Documentation Red Flags**:
- Interview notes not contemporaneously recorded
- No documented criteria for case escalation or closure
- Root cause analysis not documented

## Part 4: Implementation Recommendations

### A. Page Structure Updates

1. **Add "Auditor's Perspective" Sections**
   - Create collapsible/expandable sections under each checklist category
   - Title: "What Auditors Look For" or "Assurance Considerations"
   - Include relevant red flags from Part 3

2. **Update All CTAs**
   - Replace consulting language with assurance-focused messaging
   - Change "Need Help?" to "Ready for Assurance?"
   - Emphasise verification, evidence, documentation

3. **Add Header Context**
   - Include a statement clarifying Speeki's role as an assurance provider
   - Example: "This checklist reflects the areas independent auditors evaluate when providing assurance over your management systems."

### B. New Section: "How Auditors Evaluate This"

For each major checklist step, add a brief note on:
- What documentation auditors expect
- Common deficiencies found in audits
- Evidence that demonstrates control effectiveness

Example format:
```html
<div class="auditor-insight">
    <h4>Assurance Perspective</h4>
    <p>When providing assurance over this control, auditors typically request:</p>
    <ul>
        <li>Board meeting minutes evidencing policy approval</li>
        <li>Risk assessment methodology documentation</li>
        <li>Sample of completed due diligence files</li>
    </ul>
</div>
```

### C. Meta Description and SEO Updates

Update meta descriptions to reflect assurance positioning:


## Part 5: Tone Guidelines for Content Updates

### DO Use:

- "Auditors evaluate..."
- "Independent reviewers require..."
- "Evidence that demonstrates..."
- "Documentation standards include..."
- "Verification procedures cover..."
- "Assurance-readiness criteria..."
- "Common audit findings include..."
- "Certification bodies look for..."

### DO NOT Use:

- "We help you..."
- "Let us guide you..."
- "Our experts advise..."
- "We recommend implementing..."
- "Best practices suggest..." (unless citing a standard)
- "You should consider..."
- "Need help with..."
- "Our solutions enable you to..."
